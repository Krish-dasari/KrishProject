Business Case

The Transformational Play

McKinsey found merchants spend 40% of their time on low-value tasks: data consolidation, repetitive spreadsheet work, and performance reporting across siloed systems.

For a discount retailer with 50 category managers, that's the equivalent of 20 full-time roles doing nothing but moving data around. The Merchant Copilot deploys a squad of AI agents per category — handling signal monitoring, exception management, performance synthesis, and recommendation generation — so merchants can invest 100% of their time in the work that actually moves the business: customer strategy, category development, and vendor partnerships.

This is the most transformational of the five strategies and the highest investment — but also the one that compounds the value of everything else. A merchant who isn't drowning in spreadsheets negotiates better, builds better assortments, and creates better promotions. The Copilot is the multiplier across the entire playbook.

A Day in the Life: The Agentic Merchant

What the Agent Squad Does Autonomously

Each morning, AI agents generate a unified signal brief — a prioritized dashboard ranking performance gaps, pricing anomalies, out-of-stock risks, and markdown opportunities by P&L impact. Weekly leadership decks that once took hours to build are replaced by a 30-minute daily decision huddle where teams act on agent-recommended actions rather than debate root causes. Throughout the day, agents execute replenishment exceptions, price tests, and promo updates within approved guardrails. End-of-day, the agent reports what it acted on and what still requires human judgment — keeping merchants informed without overwhelming them.
