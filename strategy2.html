Business Case

Assortment Discipline Is Existential

For a discount retailer, the assortment is your product. Too many SKUs and you're financing working capital on slow movers, bloating planograms, and confusing shoppers. Too few and you lose basket trips to competitors with better variety. Today's assortment process is broken by design: quarterly reviews, gut-feel delistings, and new item introductions driven more by vendor pressure than data.

Agentic AI makes assortment a living system. Instead of a buyer staring at a 50,000-row spreadsheet once a quarter, agents continuously evaluate every item's velocity, margin contribution, substitution risk, and store-cluster performance. Before any delisting decision, the agent has already run the simulation: what volume moves to the next best item? What's the basket impact? What does the planogram look like in each cluster?

How the Assortment Agent Works

What the Agent Does Autonomously

The assortment agent continuously monitors item-level velocity, margin contribution, basket affinity, and planogram space utilization across every store cluster. When a tail SKU drops below velocity thresholds, the agent doesn't just flag it — it runs a full substitution impact analysis: which items absorb the volume, what's the basket bleed risk, and which store clusters are exceptions. For new items, the agent validates uplift projections against historical data from similar item launches, so buyers enter vendor conversations with objective performance expectations rather than optimistic vendor claims.

Agent Workflow: Continuous Assortment Optimization
01
Velocity & Margin Monitoring
Every SKU tracked daily against category benchmarks. Items crossing velocity floor triggers automatic investigation — not a quarterly spreadsheet review.
02
Substitution Simulation
Before any delisting, agent models customer switching behavior: what percent migrates to next-best item? What's the basket-level revenue impact? What clusters are exceptions?
03
Planogram Optimization
Space freed by delist is immediately re-allocated to highest-velocity, highest-margin items by cluster. Agent proposes planogram resets with simulated shelf productivity scores.
04
New Item Validation
Agent validates vendor uplift claims against historical data from comparable item launches. Buyers enter negotiations with objective performance expectations.
05
In-Stock Exception Management
Agent flags out-of-stock risks in the northeast region driving 35% of margin miss (McKinsey example) and coordinates with store ops for restock before sales are lost.
